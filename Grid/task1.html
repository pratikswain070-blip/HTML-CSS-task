What is the difference between a normal div and a grid container

A normal div stacks things one after another and grid container arrange items in rows and columns like a table.

Why does the layout look the same even after applying Grid?

Because we have not told how many rows and columns to be made. We said be a grid but didn't give it any rules to follow.

Are grid items created automatically?

No, the items were already there as child divs. Grid just takes those items and arranges them differently.

Can Grid work without rows or columns?

No becaue grid needs rows and columns to work without them, it is a normal container.

What do you think Grid needs to show visible changes?

Grid needs us to tell it how many columns or rows to make.
